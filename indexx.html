Total Knee Replacement

Total Knee Replacement is a surgical procedure to remove all or part of the knee joint damaged by arthritis and replace it with an artificial joint.

Treatment Overview

Total Knee Replacement (TKR) is one of the most common and successful orthopaedic surgeries performed worldwide. It involves replacing the damaged or worn-out knee joint surfaces with artificial implants to relieve pain and restore function.

In this procedure, the surgeon removes damaged cartilage and bone from the surface of the knee joint and replaces it with a prosthesis made of metal alloys, high-grade plastics, and polymers.

Total knee replacement can significantly improve quality of life, reduce pain, and restore mobility in patients suffering from severe arthritis or knee injury.

Treatment FAQs

How do I know if I am searching to receive it?
The eligibility for total knee replacement is typically determined by your orthopaedic surgeon based on your symptoms, X-rays, and medical history. If you have severe knee pain affecting daily life, it's best to consult a specialist.
I can't come alone — can a family member or friend accompany me?
Yes, absolutely. We highly recommend bringing a family member or a trusted friend to accompany you during the procedure and the initial recovery period. They can assist with mobility and post-care.
Can I see a recovery before a division and what about my post recovery?
Most patients begin walking with assistance within 24 hours of surgery. Full recovery, including return to normal daily activities, typically takes 6–12 weeks. Physical therapy plays a major role in a smooth recovery.
How do I share my medical reports and medical cases with the clinic of my choice?
You can share your reports via email or upload them directly on our patient portal. Our medical coordinator will review and connect you with the right specialist within 24–48 hours.
